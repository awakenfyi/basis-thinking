# Basis Thinking

**Every communication that moves a stranger to action carries four burdens: Tell the story, See the thing, Prove the claim, Do the next step. Basis is the grammar for reading — and building — anything against those four.**

Practiced by hand for two decades across brand, web, and product work. Now specified so a person *or an agent* can run it.

## Try it in 30 seconds

Open any homepage. Scan top to bottom and tag each section:

- **TELL** — the story and claim. *"This is what we can do for you."* (hero, H1, campaign)
- **SEE** — the thing itself. *"This is the product."* (screenshot, demo, UI)
- **PROVE** — the evidence. *"It delivers."* (logos, numbers, customers, awards)
- **DO** — the action. *"Start here."* (signup, buy, book — can attach to any module)

Now ask: which burden is missing? On most weak pages the answer takes ten seconds — usually a Tell that never Proves, or a See buried under three Tells. That gap is the whole method in miniature.

## The three layers

Role is one of **three simultaneous coordinates** every module has:

| Layer | Vocabulary | The question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Narrative role** | Tell / See / Prove / Do | What is this module doing in the story? |
| **Reader's question** | What / Why / How (+ Who else) | What is the visitor silently asking? |
| **Experience intent** | Guide / Educate / Assure / Inspire | What should they feel here? |

Each layer catches failures the others miss. The defaults run on a diagonal (Tell×Inspire, See×Educate, Prove×Assure, Do×Guide) — **the diagonal is the default; off-diagonal is stance.** A Prove that *Inspires*, a Tell that *Assures* — that's where brands differentiate, and the deviation is declared, never accidental.

## The rule

**An asset must account for all four roles.** A deliberate omission is declared in the brief — never discovered in the audit. And every Tell claim carries an asterisk that resolves to its Prove receipt *within the same asset*: the asterisk usually hides the catch; ours shows the label.
