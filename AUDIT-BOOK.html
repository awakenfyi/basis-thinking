The Basis Audit02

How to read this book

Every section of every page has a role — one of four jobs it does in the story. The thin coloured tick on each wireframe section is its role. That's the whole notation.

Tell — the story and the claim. Heroes, headers, manifestos.
See — the thing itself. Screenshots, demos, code, faces.
Prove — the evidence. Logos, quotes, numbers, stories.
Do — the ask. Buttons, forms, next steps — any tier.

Each spread gives you the page at micro scale, the module-by-module read, what's working, and Fix Monday — actions tagged to the desk that owns them: COPY DESIGN MARKETING DEV BRIEF

Two laws travel with every audit: counts, not vibes — every verdict traces to counted modules; and the asterisk law — every claim in our own copy resolves to its receipt. A page may skip a role only by declaring it; the undeclared omission is the finding.

Tell carries 39% of the estate. Prove carries 18% — and every receipt lives on one page. The reader meets claims everywhere and evidence in exactly one room.

THE FINDING

Prove goes to zero at the two decision pages. Pricing (money) and About (trust) carry no receipts while Customers hoards nine.

Fix priority — the whole estate

2One customer proof onto About, or declare the all-Tell stance in the brief
3Two of Customers' nine quotes get numbers instead of sentiment
