The Basis Audit — stripe.com03 · Copy lessons

Copy lessons — steal these

1 · Fuse the receipt into the claim
“Gamma expands to $100M ARR and 70 million users with Stripe.”
“Decagon decreases support costs by 65% with Stripe-integrated agents.”

Headlines that are simultaneously Tell and Prove — the claim arrives pre-receipted. This is the asterisk law lived at sentence level: no naked claims anywhere on the page.

2 · Outcome verbs, not category nouns
“Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue.”

The category (“financial infrastructure”) is subordinated to the reader’s outcome in the same breath. Compare the field’s pattern: category claims that stop at self-description.

3 · The honesty modifier
“99.999% historical uptime” · “50% of Fortune 100 have used Stripe”

One word each (“historical”, “have used”) that keeps a superlative technically exact. Precision as voice — claims tightened until they cannot be punctured.

4 · Concrete nouns beat adjectives
“…from Canadian yoga studios to British boxing classes…” — Mindbody testimonial

Not one adjective of praise in the strongest quote on the page — just two images. Specificity is the persuasion; the adjective is the slop tell.

5 · The scale-bracket clause
“…from your first transaction to your billionth.”

Nine words that qualify every reader at once — the startup and the enterprise hear the same sentence differently. One clause doing audience segmentation.

CTA lessons — the tier architecture, live
T1“Start now” / “Get started” / “Sign up with Google” — friction split at the same tier: two paths, one commitment level
T2“Contact sales” — always paired with T1, nav and footer, never alone
T3“Read the story” ×12 · “See the numbers” · “Get the data” — a Prove-advance economy larger than most sites’ entire CTA system
T4“Guide me” + “Find what’s right for you” — commissioning gates: the CTA that asks about YOU before selling. Rarest pattern in the field

The verdict — high level

Across 17 sections: Prove is the dominant role — inverted from the field norm (our six-page demo estate ran 39% Tell / 18% Prove; Stripe runs the mirror). Numbers appear in 9 of 17 sections. The Do system runs all four tiers simultaneously, and the T3 layer alone (“Read the story…”) is deeper than most sites’ whole CTA architecture.

THE MASTER PATTERN Stripe doesn’t put Prove near the claims — it puts Prove inside the claims. Every case-study headline, every stat, every testimonial noun is a fused Tell+Prove unit. The audit method exists because most estates separate the two; Stripe is what it looks like when a company refuses to.
THE FINDING

See is the thinnest role on the page. The hero shows a wave animation — an abstraction, not the product. The first true See (a code block, API stats) arrives 13 sections deep. The reader is told and proven at from the first pixel but doesn’t see the thing itself until the developer section. Defensible — “financial infrastructure” resists depiction, and the dashboard lives on product pages — but it is a stance, and under Basis law a stance gets declared: the omission is real, the product is invisible on the front door, and the one company famous enough to survive that is teaching everyone else a pattern they cannot afford.

What transfers — and what does not
STEALFused Tell+Prove headlines — works at any size; costs only discipline
